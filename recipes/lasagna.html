Lasagna Bolognese

Description

Lasagna Bolognese is a rich and hearty Italian dish featuring layers of wide pasta sheets, creamy béchamel sauce, and a robust Bolognese meat sauce made with ground beef, pork, tomatoes, and aromatic vegetables. Baked to perfection, it offers a comforting blend of flavors and textures, with a golden, cheesy top.

Ingredients

For the bolognese sauce:

  • Ground beef: 500g (1 lb)
  • Ground pork: 250g (½ lb)
  • Onion: 1 medium, finely chopped
  • Carrot: 1 medium, finely chopped
  • Celery: 1 stalk, finely chopped
  • Garlic: 2 cloves, minced
  • Canned tomatoes: 800g (28 oz), crushed
  • Tomato paste: 2 tablespoons
  • Milk: 1 cup
  • Olive oil: 2 tablespoons
  • Butter: 2 tablespoons
  • Salt and pepper

For the Béchamel Sauce

  • Butter: 4 tablespoons
  • Flour: 4 tablespoons
  • Milk: 4 cups
  • Salt

For the lasagna:

  • Lasagna noodles: 12-15 sheets
  • Parmesan cheese: 1 cup
  • Mozzarella cheese: 2 cups

Steps

  1. Prepare the Bolognese Sauce:
    2.
    • Sauté Vegetables: Heat olive oil and butter in a large pan over medium heat. Add the chopped onion, carrot, and celery. Cook until the vegetables are softened, about 5-7 minutes.
    • Brown the Meat: Add the ground beef and pork to the pan. Cook until browned, breaking up the meat with a spoon, about 8-10 minutes.
    • Add Garlic: Stir in the minced garlic and cook for 1 minute until fragrant.
    • Incorporate Liquids: Pour in the red wine (if using) and cook until it has mostly evaporated, about 2-3 minutes. Then add the crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, milk, bay leaf, salt, pepper, and a pinch of nutmeg. Stir well.
    • Simmer: Reduce the heat to low, cover, and let the sauce simmer for 1.5 to 2 hours, stirring occasionally. If it becomes too thick, you can add a little water or broth.

  2. Prepare the Béchamel Sauce:
    3.
    • Melt Butter: In a medium saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat.
    • Make Roux: Add the flour and stir constantly for 2-3 minutes to form a roux (a thick paste).
    • Add Milk: Gradually whisk in the milk, ensuring no lumps form. Cook, stirring constantly, until the sauce thickens and coats the back of a spoon, about 5-7 minutes.
    • Season: Add salt and a pinch of nutmeg to taste. Set aside.

  3. Cook the Lasagna Noodles:
    4.
    • Biol Noodles: If using dried lasagna noodles, cook them according to package instructions until al dente. Drain and lay them flat on a clean kitchen towel to prevent sticking. If using fresh pasta sheets, they usually do not need pre-cooking.

  4. Assemble the Lasagna:
    5.
    • Preheat Oven: Preheat your oven to 180°C (350°F).
    • Grease Dish: Butter a large baking dish.
    • First Layer: Spread a thin layer of Bolognese sauce on the bottom of the dish.
    • Layering: Place a layer of lasagna noodles over the sauce. Spread a layer of Bolognese sauce over the noodles, followed by a layer of béchamel sauce. Sprinkle with a little grated Parmesan cheese.
    • Repeat: Continue layering in this order: noodles, Bolognese sauce, béchamel sauce, and Parmesan. You should have 3-4 layers, ending with a layer of béchamel sauce.
    • Top Layer: Sprinkle generously with the remaining Parmesan cheese and mozzarella if using.

  5. Bake the Lasagna:
    6.
    • Bake: Cover the dish with aluminum foil and bake for 25 minutes.
    • Brown the Top: Remove the foil and bake for an additional 20-25 minutes, or until the top is golden and bubbling.
    • Rest: Let the lasagna rest for 10-15 minutes before slicing and serving.
